Overview

In July 2018, the Hall of Planet Earth was updated in key areas to reflect scientific advances. This included a permanent and updatable exhibit on climate change titled What causes climate and climate change? with the goals to maintain the hall's status as a preeminent visitor destination and teaching hall as well as leverage new technologies to enhance visitor experience.

Physical layout

Three areas of the hall were updated during this renovation, however, this guide will initially focus on the Climate & climate change section.

Areas of project focus

The Climate & climate change section is located in the south east corner of the hall that is accessible from both entrances. It is a 36 foot media wall comprised of 36 high-definition 55-inch screens.

Climate wall elevation model

The top three rows of screens show "big picture" key messages about climate change (this is internally referred to as the "meta message" section.) The bottom row of screens are interactive screens that provide deeper engagement with the content. Below the screens are the graphic panels which provide context and expands on the interactive content. The graphic panels also have embedded interactive physical controls (sliders, dials, buttons) that control the interactives above to create a natural connection between the panels and screens.

Studying past climates elevation model

Opposite the media wall is the Studying past climates section which includes a model of an ice core with an updated sliding screen interactive as well as fully updated graphic panels.
